## Analyzed Games

### Advanced D&D: Eye of the Beholder
- **Combat System**: Strategic positioning allows free hits on monsters
- **Information Design**: Limited HP info creates interesting challenge in party management
- **Magic System**: "Petition" for spells which characters auto-cast while resting

### Pokemon Red
- **Progression**: Evolution creates rewarding development path
- **Exploration**: Finding rare Pokemon makes exploration rewarding
- **Aesthetics**: Good enough graphics, memorable music

### Smash Bros Series
- **64**: Flow-inducing gameplay, rolls, side dodge, double jump, wall cling, recovery moves
- **Melee**: [Notes to be added]

### Paper Mario
- **Dungeon Design**: Infinite dungeon with strong reward mechanisms

### Ultima Online
- **Skill System**: Anatomy, magery, snooping
- **Player Economy**: Stealing from other players
- **Environment**: Deep, scary dungeons with difficult monsters
- **Combat Strategy**: Exploiting weaknesses (e.g., killing titans with poison)
- **Progression**: Slow but satisfying character growth, skill training per use (intuitive)
- **World Design**: Varied world with distinct towns
- **Spell System**: Paralyze, polymorph, summon (horse, blade spirits, energy vortex, greater demon), poison, teleport, mark, recall, gate
- **Death Mechanics**: Ghost form with unique potential

### Diablo 1
- **Progression**: Experience/leveling system
- **Rewards**: Finding spellbooks, altars with potential gains

### Princess.Loot.Repeat
- **Item System**: Artifact combinations
- **Character Development**: Progressive power scaling
- **Rewards**: High-value +1 stat items creating significant impact

### MagicMaker
- **Customization**: Deep customization elements
- **Abilities**: Teleport, seeking, piercing, split shot, turret
- **Economy**: Good use of scarcity to enhance rewards

### Diablo 2
- **Item System**: Uniques, sets, runewords, socketing, stat rerolls
- **Character Development**: Skill trees with synergies (ensures different playthroughs)
- **Crafting**: Runewords, passive items, sets

### Disgaea
- **Progression**: Extreme level caps
- **Item Development**: Leveling items
- **Gameplay Variety**: Puzzles
- **Tone**: Humor
- **Engagement**: Uniquely addictive progression loops

### Spelunky
- **Items**: Jetpack
- **Interactions**: Shop robberies
- **Level Design**: Secret areas

### Final Fantasy 5
- **Class System**: Job system
- **Unique Classes**: Blue Mage
- **Customization**: Dual classing

### Final Fantasy Tactics
- **Job System**: Diverse classes including Squire, Chemist, Arithmetician, Knight, White Mage, Dark Knight, Archer, Black Mage, Mime, Monk, Mystic, Ninja, Thief, TimeMage, Onion Knight, Dragoon, Orator, Samurai, Geomancer, Summoner, Dancer, Bard
- **Mechanics**: Stealing, tactical positioning

### Terraria
- **Crafting**: Advanced crafting system
- **Items**: Diverse and powerful items
- **Movement**: Mounts
- **World Design**: Open world exploration

### Rogue Legacy
- **Genre Fusion**: Side-scrolling rogue-like RPG
- **Progression**: New Game+ mechanics

### ADOM (Ancient Domains of Mystery)
- **Special Mechanics**: Wishes (including early-game pool wish)
- **Items**: Potions (troll's blood, gain attribute), holy water, girdle of giant strength, cloak of invisibility, rings of regeneration, artifacts, eternium tower shields, scrolls of education
- **Classes**: Mindcrafter, Monk (fast movement), Beastfighter, Wizard, Duelist (with enchanted phase dagger), Halfling Archer, Troll Healer
- **Races**: Quickling, Demons, Golems, Elementals, Ogres, Giants, Titans, Dragons, Ghouls
- **Spells**: Magic map, teleport, fireball, magic shovel, farsight, heal, acid ball
- **Economy**: Shops with rare items (DELUX books, ring of djinni summoning), potions, shop raids
- **Difficulty**: Steep learning curve creating replayability
- **Atmosphere**: Tense/scary environments
- **Unique Mechanics**: Stat gain through eating corpses
- **Monster Design**: Diverse and challenging
- **Stat System**: Strength (damage, hit chance, carry capacity), Dexterity (hit chance, DV, speed), Toughness (HP, PV), Willpower (spell impact), Perception (vision range), Learning, Mana (spell damage), Charisma (prices and companions), Appearance (prices and chaos resistance), Speed, PV, DV, HP, MP
- **Progression**: Feats, weapon training, skills
- **Skills**: Swimming, detect traps, dodge, regeneration, archery, alchemy, athletics, concentration, literacy, herbalism
- **Core Mechanics**: Speed + energy, corruption, shields, birthsigns
- **Design Philosophy**: Complex enough for proper customization, no useless elements
- **Replayability**: Each playthrough different but with player agency
- **Exploration**: Water features, vaults, rewarding locations (strange dungeon, dwarftown, chaosforge, bug temple, underwater cavern, casino)
- **Drawbacks**: Heavy RNG for item drops, limited alchemy and enchanting

### Binding of Isaac
- **Level Design**: Gold rooms
- **Risk/Reward**: Devil pacts
- **Items**: Powerful artifact combinations (homing lasers)

### Elder Scrolls: Morrowind
- **Crafting**: Overpowered enchanting and alchemy systems
- **Movement**: Acrobatics skills (jumping on houses, over walls)
- **Level Design**: Diverse dungeons
- **Combat**: Archery system
- **Character Development**: Meditation mechanic
- **Cons**: Limited replayability

### Legend of Zelda Series
- **Ocarina of Time**: Immense, beautiful world
- **Original**: Rewarding exploration, progression through items, automapping, memorable music
- **Zelda II**: Music, leveling system

### NetHack
- **Items**: Enemy-sensing necklace

### Caves of Qud
- **Technology**: Solar-celled enginrifle turrets
- **Character Development**: Mutations (domination, temporal clones, multiple limbs, teleport, foresight, mind blast)
- **Build Variety**: Adrenal 4-legged temporal clones, sense enemy phase raider
- **Items**: Shotgun, carbine
- **Skills**: Tinkering

### Dwarf Fortress
- **Combat System**: Detailed limb damage
- **AI**: Emergent behavior
- **Construction**: Building options (above/below ground, mushroom structures, towers, labyrinths)
- **Defenses**: Drawbridges, traps

### RimWorld
- **Economy**: Slavery, organ harvesting
- **Skill Development**: Training medicine through leg replacements
- **Technology**: Cybernetic implants, turrets
- **Survival**: Food growth, trap mechanisms

### Stoneshard
- **Survival Systems**: Complex bio-mental reactions (hunger, thirst, morale, sanity, pain, intoxication)
- **Injury System**: Limb-specific injuries
- **Risk/Reward**: High-stakes gameplay
- **Items**: Rare, valuable spellbooks
